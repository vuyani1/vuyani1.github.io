Most Hated Video Games (2000–2025)
2000: Daikatana – John Romero’s highly-anticipated shooter became infamous for failing its hype. After multiple delays it shipped in poor shape, with confusing level designs and an AI “buddy” that impeded play. Critics slammed its outdated graphics and bugs, and Computer Gaming World even named it the “worst game of 2000”
en.wikipedia.org
. Fans cite its broken gameplay and over‑hyped marketing (notably featuring a talking swordfish) as major failings
game-over.com
en.wikipedia.org
.
2001: Kabuki Warriors – This Xbox fighting game by Genki was widely panned. GameSpot explicitly awarded it “Worst Game of 2001” due to clumsy controls and absurd character design
en.wikipedia.org
. Reviewers noted its stiff animations, dreadful balance and baffling mechanics. Fans consider it one of the most ridiculous and unplayable fighting games, with virtually no redeeming qualities
en.wikipedia.org
.
2002: Mortal Kombat: Advance – The Game Boy Advance MK port is often cited as a nadir for the franchise. It compiled an aggregated Metacritic score around 33/100 and drew universal scorn
en.wikipedia.org
. Critics lambasted its terrible controls, choppy sprite animation and missing features from arcade versions. IGN summed it up as “an absolute embarrassment” for the series
en.wikipedia.org
, making it a top candidate for the era’s worst game.
2003: Big Rigs: Over the Road Racing – This budget PC racer is infamous as one of the worst games ever made. It shipped in an unplayable state: trucks could drive off-road or even through bridges with no collision detection, the sole “opponent” never moved, and the game ended with the message “YOU’RE WINNER!”. GameSpot refused to give it a score calling it “the worst game [they had] ever seen”
en.wikipedia.org
. It earned the lowest Metacritic score (around 8/100) of any game and is legendary for its broken physics and lack of meaningful gameplay
en.wikipedia.org
.
2004: The Guy Game – A crude adult trivia game for PS2/Xbox, it became notorious for tone‑deaf content and a legal scandal. The gameplay itself was simplistic and repetitive, but the real backlash came after release: one contestant sued when footage showed she was underage, forcing publishers to pull the game
en.wikipedia.org
. Critics panned its sexist, “inane” questions and trivia, calling it a “lackluster” experience with “inane” humor
en.wikipedia.org
. The resulting controversy and poor design make it a black mark on 2004.
2005: Lula 3D – A PC erotic adventure that was universally derided. Lula 3D earned abysmal review scores (around 28% on Metacritic) thanks to its monotonous gameplay, crude humor, and dreadful graphics
en.wikipedia.org
. Eurogamer gave it a 2/10, criticizing its “ugly and offensive” approach, and PC Zone awarded 3.1/10
en.wikipedia.org
. Players condemned it as repetitively dull with cheap sexual jokes and technical sloppiness
en.wikipedia.org
.
2006: Bomberman: Act Zero – This bold, gritty reimagining of Bomberman on Xbox 360 bombed hard. Reviewers “panned” it across the board: Metacritic sits at ~34/100 and many called it “one of the worst games ever made”
en.wikipedia.org
. The redesign swapped Bomberman’s charm for drab metallic arenas and dull multiplayer. Critics cited its poor level design, clumsy controls and generic aesthetic as evidence of a misguided reboot
en.wikipedia.org
.
2007: The Shield – A licensed tie-in beat-’em-up based on the TV cop drama. It was a critical flop: PlayStation 2 and PC versions hold scores in the mid-30s on Metacritic. Critics slammed the unresponsive combat, repetitive missions and technical glitches
en.wikipedia.org
. IGN said it was a “lackluster trivia game” with “inane humor” (in the context of The Guy Game review), but similarly for The Shield one could say it felt like “tack-on” content with serious bugs
en.wikipedia.org
. Fans largely consider it a wasted license effort
en.wikipedia.org
.
2008: (No single game widely identified) — Our sources did not highlight a definitive “most hated” title for 2008. While some players recall hated titles like Alien Syndrome (PSP) or Rogue Warriors, no cited source singled out one game that year as the worst. In general, 2008’s mainstream releases were solid, so the absence of a consensus "worst game of 2008" in sources suggests no clear standout flop from that year.
2009: Leisure Suit Larry: Box Office Bust – A long-shot revival of Larry Laffer turned disaster. Its 2009 release got only about 17/100 on Metacritic
en.wikipedia.org
. Reviewers lambasted its empty world, dated graphics and technical bugs, and IGN gave it an unprecedented zero recommendation
en.wikipedia.org
. GameSpot, ScrewAttack and others labeled it the worst game of 2009
en.wikipedia.org
. Fans criticized its incoherent story, tedious mini-games and endless glitches
en.wikipedia.org
en.wikipedia.org
.
2010: Final Fantasy XIV (Original Release) – The initial launch of FFXIV on PC was a notorious failure. It felt unfinished and grindy: critics complained of poor UI, repetitive quests and numerous bugs, culminating in a Metacritic around 49/100
en.wikipedia.org
. Square Enix eventually refunded subscribers and relaunched the title as “A Realm Reborn.” In the interim, players openly rejected the original for its shallow content and stability issues
en.wikipedia.org
.
2011: Postal III – The third entry in the violent Postal series is widely reviled. It shipped with a litany of technical problems and design missteps, earning “generally unfavorable” reviews (around 24/100 Metacritic)
en.wikipedia.org
. Game Informer gave it 1/10 and GameSpot 3/10
en.wikipedia.org
, with criticisms of “unstable” programming and juvenile writing. Even some PC Gamer and GameSpot reviewers called it one of the lowest-rated games ever
en.wikipedia.org
. Fans derided its cartoony violence and constant crashing as evidence it should never have left alpha.
2012: (No single game widely identified) — Our sources did not pinpoint a standout worst game of 2012. Some players later mentioned titles like Infestation: Survivor Stories (a controversial MMO), but without authoritative citations we cannot name a “most hated” game for this year. No major industry outlets labeled any 2012 release with that distinction in our sources.
2013: SimCity (2013)

 – The reboot of SimCity became infamous almost overnight. Its always-online requirement led to severe server crashes and delays at launch, leaving many buyers unable to play at all
pcgamer.com
. Players endured endless loading and disconnections under EA’s cloud servers. Critics and writers describe its launch as “heartbreaking,” with “frequent crashes, extreme latency, exceedingly long load times, disconnections” before city-building could even begin
pcgamer.com
. The fiasco prompted refunds and an apology from Maxis, cementing SimCity 2013’s reputation as the year’s most hated due to its botched online requirements
pcgamer.com
.
2014: Assassin’s Creed Unity – Ubisoft’s next-gen Paris stealth game launched with crippling bugs. It was marred by “widespread game-breaking bugs, glitches, degraded performance and online issues”
en.wikipedia.org
. Paris’s famous landmarks could sometimes vanish or flicker, crowds would disintegrate, and many missions became unfinishable due to glitches. Ubisoft apologized publicly, patched heavily, and even gave away free DLC to compensate
en.wikipedia.org
. The technical mess severely damaged Unity’s reputation and “some would say it was the worst AC launch ever” due to the unplayable launch state
en.wikipedia.org
.
2015: Alone in the Dark: Illumination – This PC and console action-horror entry was universally panned. Critics gave it abysmal scores (19% on GameRankings, 19/100 Metacritic)
en.wikipedia.org
. Many outlets flatly called it “the worst game of 2015.” It drew ire for its horrid graphics, broken co-op, and laughable “light weapon” mechanic. James Stephanie Sterling summarized: it was “ugly in every sense of the word” and even awarded it Worst Game of the Year
en.wikipedia.org
. Players found its levels punishingly dull and full of glitches, making it a poster child for incompetent development.
2016: No Man’s Sky – Hello Games’ space exploration title launched to a storm of backlash. Pre-release trailers had promised features that weren’t present at launch. On release, players found “no multiplayer” and many missing promised mechanics
en.wikipedia.org
. Early reviews noted a serene but shallow universe where most expected variety was absent. By the end of 2016, user reviews flipped to “overwhelmingly negative,” and critics warned it stands as a cautionary tale of over-hyped marketing
en.wikipedia.org
.
2017: Star Wars Battlefront II – This EA shooter came under intense fire for its monetization. The pre-launch beta revealed that unlocking iconic heroes (like Luke Skywalker) required absurd grinding or buying lootboxes, effectively making it pay-to-win. Within days of its November 2017 release, EA’s stock dropped ~$3 billion due to fan revolt
en.wikipedia.org
. The backlash was so severe that EA temporarily disabled microtransactions and apologized. While the gameplay was fine, the loot-crate controversy (and its anticonsumer practices) earned it “hated game” status among players
en.wikipedia.org
.
2018: Fallout 76 – Bethesda’s online Fallout spin-off was a disaster at launch. It was riddled with bugs, had no NPCs or story content (initially), and even the premium Collector’s Edition shipped with a cheap nylon bag instead of promised canvas
businessinsider.com
. Players encountered game-breaking glitches and long-standing “nuclear” exploits. Fans demanded refunds en masse, and a class-action suit was even discussed over its broken launch and misleading packaging
businessinsider.com
businessinsider.com
. In short, Fallout 76 became infamous for its broken gameplay and Bethesda’s refusal to fix or refund early on
businessinsider.com
businessinsider.com
.
2019: (No single game widely identified) — We found no clear consensus “most hated” game for 2019 in our sources. Some mobile or mid-tier games may have drawn ire, but no major console/PC title stood out in the research. (Notably, WWE 2K20 did receive criticism for bugs, but no source here labels it definitively worst of that year.)
2020: Warcraft III: Reforged – Blizzard’s remaster of Warcraft III promised updated graphics but shipped half-baked. Many old features and promised upgrades were missing or poorly implemented. Ars Technica called it the “most staggering and baffling disappointment” of 2020
en.wikipedia.org
. Fans and reviewers lambasted its downgraded art (some new units looked worse than before) and constant disconnects in online play. By February 2020 Blizzard was already offering no-questions refunds, acknowledging it had failed to meet expectations
en.wikipedia.org
.
2021: eFootball 2022 – Konami’s rebranded PES series launch was a notorious failure. eFootball 2022 received over 90% negative reviews on Steam immediately
en.wikipedia.org
. Critics described its graphics as “atrocious,” and there were glaring animation and AI issues. The game was released in an unfinished, content-starved state with finicky controls, leading Konami to admit it launched “incomplete”
en.wikipedia.org
. It briefly held the title of lowest-rated game on Steam upon release due to this botched launch
en.wikipedia.org
.
2022: Babylon’s Fall – This Square Enix action-RPG bombed completely. It quickly became one of the year’s lowest-rated games. Babylon’s Fall was reported as the “third-worst game of 2022” on Metacritic, and its playerbase collapsed within days. At launch, it peaked under 1,200 concurrent Steam players then fell to just one or two
en.wikipedia.org
. Critics cited bland combat and constant bugs. Square Enix shut down the servers within months. Its abysmal reception and lightning-fast demise make it a textbook hated game.
2023: The Lord of the Rings: Gollum – Daedalic’s stealth-platformer was widely derided immediately upon release. Reviewers panned “literally everything” about it. Bugs and controls are the main culprits: players report Gollum often fails to respond to inputs, causing repeated frustrating deaths, and the game is described as “riddled with bugs”
kotaku.com
. Critics noted many glitches, crashes and tedious design, with Kotaku bluntly declaring it “2023’s worst game”. Fans especially hated its broken platforming and amateurish tech, making it infamously disastrous.
2024: (No single game widely identified) — Our sources did not clearly single out a “most hated” game of 2024. Some big releases like Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League drew criticism for bugs and microtransactions, but no mainstream outlet in our sources declared a definitive worst game for the year.
2025: MindsEye – Released in early 2025 by ex-GTA producer Leslie Benzies, it earned immediate scorn. Kotaku described it as a “day-one car wreck” plagued by bugs and poor optimization
kotaku.com
. Early players report rampant glitches and unresponsive gameplay – one wrote “it plays like crap”
kotaku.com
. With only ~40% positive Steam reviews at launch, it was drawing comparisons to Cyberpunk 2077’s disaster. Reported issues include face-animation bugs, NPCs not reacting, and frequent crashes
kotaku.com
. This broken first effort has cemented MindsEye as arguably the most reviled 2025 release so far
kotaku.com
.
